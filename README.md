 ## How I Would Present Our Findings

#### Background Info
Today, I am going to go over my entire process for analyzing our sales data to provide practical insights. Starting with the raw data, I noticed two things. Some quantity values were less than 1, and some product prices were negative. To begin my analysis, I first had to clean the data. By filtering out incorrect values, I could ensure the data would be of excellent quality and reliable enough to continue.

#### Seasonality
I have created four visualizations to help us make better decisions in the future. Let's begin with the first visualization, the revenue by month for 2011. Based on the chart, we can see that revenue is range-bound from January to the end of August. After a slow start to the year, we can see that revenue picks up a bit by March, then drops off in April. During the summer, revenue remains around mid-700 thousand. 

Near the end of quarter 3, revenue begins to pick up rapidly. Holidays are around the corner, and this is where we start to see a lot of success. From August to November, revenue spiked by nearly 800,000 dollars, reaching a peak of just below 1.5 million. Revenue remained strong until the end of the year and the end of the holiday season.

#### Top Countries
Let's move on to the second visualization, the top 10 countries by revenue. Excluding the United Kingdom, the top 10 countries are: the Netherlands, Ireland, Germany, France, Australia, Sweden, Switzerland, Spain, Belgium, and Norway. Using a clustered column chart, we can see each country's total quantity and total revenue. The light blue bars represent quantity, and the dark blue bars represent revenue. Despite the Netherlands purchasing the 200 thousand products, its revenue isn't far off Ireland's, which bought 143 thousand items. Germany and France are nearly identical in terms of quantity and revenue. Sweden, Switzerland, Spain, Belgium, and Norway are also very similar in terms of quantity and revenue. **Ireland, Germany, France, Switzerland, Spain, and Norway seem to be purchasing more expensive products compared to the other countries, so it may be worth targeting them for more sales.**

#### Top Customers 
From the third visualization, we can see our top 10 customers by revenue in descending order. The top 3 customers alone generate over $700,000 in revenue. Customers 4 through 7 taper off a bit, ranging from 138,000 to 88,000. Customers 8 through 10 are nearly identical, with revenue ranging from around 66 thousand to 65 thousand dollars. **It’s important to target top customers to ensure that they remain satisfied with the quality of our products and keep them purchasing from us.**

#### Top Regions
Lastly, let’s have a look at the top regions by revenue, excluding the United Kingdom once again. Europe is the top region by far, and we can see that from the map. Nearly the entirety of Europe is covered by circles which represent revenue. The larger the circles, the larger the revenue. Other top regions include Australia and Japan. Smaller but notable regions include: the United States, Canada, Brazil, south Asia, and the Republic of South Africa.
